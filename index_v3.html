Nhân Phan

Hi, this is Nhan. I base mostly in Vietnam and work accross media art, programming, and teaching.



PRACTICE ⎯
Born to the era of the Internet, the computer has long been my friend. My art practice is an inquiry into that relationship between me (human) and my friend (computer). Less technical and more humane, my works often revolve around romance, family, culture, and identity. These themes extend across mediums including programming, mixed-media installation, photography, prints, and letters. My work has been showcased in New York, Hong Kong, Bangkok, Hanoi, and Ho Chi Minh City. I am a finalist of the Lumen Prize 2025.

Recently, I have been working on an interactive film installation with the RMIT Digital and Art Grant, and an archive of Vietnamese folklore with the Goethe-Institut Ho Chi Minh City. I also have an upcoming residency with AirHue in January 2026.



TECHNICALITY ⎯
I have a background in market research and have worked extensively with data and statistics. This foundation has allowed me to move further into machine learning. My interests lie in databases, learning algorithms, and computer vision. I am a TensorFlow-certified developer, also fluent in full-stack web development and physical computing.

I earned my first bachelor’s degree in Marketing at Ritsumeikan Asia Pacific University (Japan). My second bachelor’s degree was in Computer Science at Goldsmiths, University of London (UK), where I majored in Machine Learning & Artificial Intelligence.



TEACHING ⎯
I am the founder and teacher at CodeSurfing, the first art-tech collective in Vietnam, advocating for accessibility in technology for local artists. I teach primarily in p5.js. In 2023, I was a Teaching Fellow of the Processing Foundation Fellowship, and in 2024, I continued as a mentor. Prior to this, from 2020 to 2023, I worked as a teaching assistant and later as an instructor at CoderSchool, where I led classes and workshops covering data analysis with Python, computer vision, and generative networks.



CONTACT ⎯
You can reach me via my email. My teaching and programs are public via CodeSurfing (IG and Web).
